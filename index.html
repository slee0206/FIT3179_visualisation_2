Recycled vs Disposed

There are many types of waste produced in Australia, where not all of them are recyclable. For example, Hazard waste is one of the most types of waste produced in Australia and majority of the hazardous waste is being disposed. However, we can see that majority of the waste has higher recycled waste than disposed, which is a great sign.

Variation

In Australia, residents and industrials do put effort is distinguish in types of waste, such as having bins for food waste, recyclable and disposable. In this way we were able to get clear data on which types of waste we should be reducing.

Over the years

Exploring the statistics on previous decade worth of data, we find that overall recycled and disposed rate are increasing, considering the fact the awareness of using recyclable products and materials, the amount of disposed increased in a slower rate compared to the recycled.

The three main streams of our waste are from...

C&D (Construction & Demolition)

Waste generated from construction and demolition activities, which include concrete, wood, and metal.

C&I (Commercial & Industrial)

Waste generated by commercial and industrial operations, including plastics, metals, and packaging materials.

State-by-state

This chart highlights the waste distribution by type across Australian states and territories. Each bar represents a different state, showing how various waste types contribute to the overall waste in each region. Significant waste types vary from state to state, with some states producing more industrial or construction waste than others.

Major waste types

Across the states, construction waste, organics, and paper/cardboard are consistently high in volume. The chart provides insights into the prevalent waste types, such as high levels of organics in urban areas compared to industrial waste, which is more common in resource-rich regions.

Patterns

The data suggests certain trends over time. For instance, regions with higher industrial activities show increased levels of metal and glass waste. Meanwhile, states focusing on recycling show a gradual increase in organics and paper recycling efforts.
